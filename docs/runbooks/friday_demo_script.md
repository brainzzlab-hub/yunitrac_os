## What I claim (proofable)
- Deterministic equality on declared sets (Enterprise B==C buckets/hashes; Lite REQUIRED_EQUALITY_SET).
- No-feedback bounded outputs (SecCode-only); AUDIT/SECURITY content-free; METRICS numeric-only bounded/quantized.
- Audit chain verification (Enterprise) fail-closed.
- Approvals: unsigned rejected, signed accepted (PKCS#11), nonce==0/duplicate rejected.
- Diode-first (Enterprise) / soft-diode enforced by gates (Lite).

## What I do NOT claim
- No performance/SLA guarantees.
- No host OS hardening beyond documented sandbox wrapper.
- Lite does not claim PKCS#11 chain verification or hardware diode.
- No data-at-rest encryption promise.
- No reasons or validator internals exposed.

## Questions & safe answers
- Q: “Why PASS?” A: PASS indicates deterministic checks and policy gates succeeded; details are in the evidence bundle hashes.
- Q: “Why REJECT?” A: We report only bounded SecCode; we do not expose validator reasons.
- Q: “Can I get logs?” A: Boundary profiles avoid stdout/stderr; evidence is via declared artifacts only.
- Q: “What about timestamps?” A: Equality-scoped artifacts are timestamp-free by design.

## Failure handling
- If any command fails: stop, show the failing PASS/FAIL line only, do not speculate on reasons. Offer to rerun after offline triage.
- Do not reveal validator reasons or internal errors; refer to SecCode only if emitted.
